# Horizon Europe Strategic Orientations Map

This synthesis maps the three overarching strategic orientations of the [[Horizon Europe Strategic Plan 2025-2027]] to the programme's architecture and policy ecosystem.

---

## The Three Strategic Orientations

### 1. The Green Transition

**Policy drivers**: [[European Green Deal]], [[Clean Industrial Deal]], European Climate Law, Nature Restoration Regulation, European Water Resilience Strategy, European Ocean Pact

**Budget allocation**: at least 35% of HE funding to climate objectives (EUR 4,903M in 2026-2027); at least 10% to [[Biodiversity]] (EUR 1,661M)

**Primary clusters**:
| Cluster | Climate contribution |
|---------|---------------------|
| [[Cluster 5 Climate Energy and Mobility]] | 89.7% (EUR 1,644M) |
| [[Cluster 6 Food Bioeconomy Natural Resources Agriculture and Environment]] | 66.1% (EUR 778M) |
| [[Cluster 3 Civil Security for Society]] | 28.6% (EUR 96M) |
| [[Cluster 4 Digital Industry and Space]] | 22.6% (EUR 437M) |
| [[Cluster 1 Health]] | 16.6% (EUR 176M) |
| [[Cluster 2 Culture Creativity and Inclusive Society]] | 10.0% (EUR 64M) |

**Cross-cutting mechanisms**: [[Do No Significant Harm]] principle, [[EU Missions]] (Climate Adaptation, Smart Cities, Oceans, Soil), [[Circular Economy]] actions, [[New European Bauhaus]]

**Horizontal calls**: [[Clean Industrial Deal]] (EUR 540M) — decarbonisation of energy-intensive industries + clean technologies for climate action

---

### 2. The Digital Transition

**Policy drivers**: AI Continent Action Plan, European Strategy for AI in Science, Digital Decade targets, [[Competitiveness Compass]]

**Budget allocation**: 33% of all HE funds to digital transition; EUR 13B minimum to core digital technologies over HE lifetime; EUR 2,023M to AI in 2026-2027; estimated 35.8% of cluster/RI/WPERA/EIE/Missions/NEB/Horizontal funds contribute to AI

**Primary clusters**: [[Cluster 4 Digital Industry and Space]] (lead), with AI integration across all clusters

**Cross-cutting mechanisms**: [[AI in Science]] horizontal call (EUR 90M + EUR 775M across WP), [[European Open Science Cloud]], digital health, precision agriculture, smart mobility

**Key research themes**: [[Artificial Intelligence]], [[Quantum Technologies]], cybersecurity, digital twins, space technologies

---

### 3. A More Resilient, Competitive, Inclusive, and Democratic Europe

**Policy drivers**: [[Competitiveness Compass]], [[Open Strategic Autonomy]], ProtectEU Strategy, European Preparedness Union Strategy, European Economic Security Strategy

**Budget allocation**: EUR 634M to [[Critical Raw Materials]]; EUR 240M for ERA Chairs; EUR 870M for knowledge valorisation

**Primary clusters**: [[Cluster 3 Civil Security for Society]] (security), [[Cluster 2 Culture Creativity and Inclusive Society]] (democracy, inclusion), all clusters (competitiveness)

**Cross-cutting mechanisms**: [[Choose Europe for Science]], [[Widening Participation]], [[International Cooperation]], [[European Partnerships]] (9 new partnerships in strategic plan), European Startup and Scaleup Hubs

**Key policies**: [[Gender Equality]], [[SSH Integration]], [[Social Innovation]], [[Societal Readiness]]

---

## Strategic Convergence Points

Several entities sit at the intersection of multiple orientations:

1. **[[Clean Industrial Deal]]**: green transition + competitiveness + digital (clean tech + industry + AI)
2. **[[AI in Science]]**: digital transition + competitiveness + green (AI for materials, agriculture, climate)
3. **[[European Partnerships]]**: all three orientations through sectoral partnerships
4. **[[EU Missions]]**: green + resilience + digital (cities, health, climate, nature)
5. **[[Open Strategic Autonomy]]**: competitiveness + security + digital sovereignty

## Detected Strategic Narratives

- **From activity-driven to impact-driven**: paradigm shift in programme design (destinations, expected outcomes, Key Impact Pathways)
- **Growing convergence of green and digital**: AI for climate, digital twins for energy, smart agriculture
- **Horizontal calls as policy instruments**: CID and AI in Science break cluster silos
- **Simplification as strategy**: fewer topics (-35%), shorter WP (-33%), lump sums (50%), two-stage calls (41), less prescriptive descriptions
- **Talent competition**: Choose Europe for Science reflects geopolitical competition for researchers
- **Supply chain sovereignty**: CRM, advanced materials, quantum — reducing dependencies
