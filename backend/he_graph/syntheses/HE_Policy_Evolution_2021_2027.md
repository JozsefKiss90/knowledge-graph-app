# Horizon Europe Policy Evolution 2021-2027

This page tracks how Horizon Europe has evolved across its lifetime, based on the Programme Guide version history and the shift from the first to the second strategic plan.

---

## Key Policy Shifts

### 1. From Activity-Driven to Impact-Driven (2021)
The defining paradigm shift of Horizon Europe. Work programmes organised around 'Destinations' (expected impacts) and 'Topics' (expected outcomes), replacing the activity-driven structure of Horizon 2020. This is reinforced through Key Impact Pathways tracking 9 story lines.

### 2. Geopolitical Tightening (2022-2025)
- China excluded from Innovation Actions (V2.0, 2022)
- Russia/Belarus/non-government Ukraine territories excluded from all participation (V3.0+)
- Russia-specific restrictive measures expanded via Council Regulation 2024/1745 (V5.0)
- [[Open Strategic Autonomy]] participation restrictions strengthened
- 22 countries now associated to HE

### 3. Gender Mainstreaming Deepened (2022-2025)
- GEP eligibility criterion introduced (calls from 2022)
- Gender dimension in R&I content mandatory by default
- Gender balance as ranking criterion
- EIC gender targets (40% women-led pitches, 50% women in advisory)
- Three gender categories (woman, man, non-binary)
- Intersectionality framework introduced

### 4. Simplification Acceleration (2026-2027)
- Topic descriptions shortened (-33% WP length)
- Fewer topics (-35%)
- Single-project topics reduced by 50%
- [[Lump Sum Funding]] expanded to 50% of call budget
- [[Two-Stage Calls]] expanded to 41 topics with anonymised first-stage
- Reduced proposal template complexity

### 5. Horizontal Calls Innovation (2026-2027)
- [[Clean Industrial Deal]] horizontal call (EUR 540M) — first cross-cluster industry call
- [[AI in Science]] horizontal call (EUR 90M) — first cross-cluster AI call
- Bottom-up, non-prescriptive, open topic design

### 6. Open Science Maturation (2021-2025)
- V5.0-5.1: updated open science section
- Moved beyond open access to comprehensive open science framework
- FAIR principles operationalised through [[European Open Science Cloud]]
- Research data management (RDM) mandatory; DMPs by month 6
- Removed obsolete RDM plan requirements (V5.0)

### 7. Societal Readiness Formalisation (2025)
- New Part 11 added in V5.0, recognising [[Societal Readiness]] as a distinct cross-cutting dimension alongside [[SSH Integration]] and [[Social Innovation]]
- Builds on Responsible Research and Innovation (RRI) from Horizon 2020

## Strategic Plan Transition: 2021-2024 to 2025-2027

| Dimension | First Strategic Plan (2021-2024) | Second Strategic Plan (2025-2027) |
|-----------|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Strategic orientations | 4 orientations | 3 orientations (consolidated) |
| Partnerships | Existing portfolio | 9 new partnerships announced |
| Biodiversity | General commitment | 10% budget target |
| AI | Growing emphasis | Dedicated horizontal call + EUR 2B |
| Industrial policy | Green Deal focus | Clean Industrial Deal + Competitiveness Compass |
| Talent | General mobility | Choose Europe for Science initiative |
| Simplification | Lump sums piloted | 50% lump sums, two-stage calls, shorter WP |
