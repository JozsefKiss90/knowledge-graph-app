# Overview
Cluster 5 'Climate, Energy and Mobility' operates under Pillar II of [[Horizon Europe]]. It supports R&I on decarbonising the economy and society, promoting sustainable production and consumption, and advancing knowledge on climate science. It identifies effective pathways, cross-cutting technologies, and solutions to address climate change mitigation and adaptation, energy needs, and mobility challenges.

# Strategic Role
This cluster is the primary vehicle for the EU's green transition within the R&I framework. It focuses on affordable and secure energy supplies, sustainable mobility, industrial decarbonisation, circularity in manufacturing, and closing the skills gap for competitiveness.

# Policy Context
- Aligned with the [[European Green Deal]] and climate neutrality by 2050
- Supports the [[Clean Industrial Deal]] through clean technology deployment
- The Industrial Action Plan for the European automotive sector is supported through multiple actions including a flagship topic on connected, cooperative, and automated mobility
- Three European co-programmed partnerships linked to the automotive sector (2Zero, CCAM, BATT4EU)

# Horizon Europe Relevance
- Budget contribution to climate action: 89.7% (EUR 1,644 million) — the highest among all clusters
- Also co-finances the [[Clean Industrial Deal]] horizontal call under Horizontal Activities
- Digital technologies leveraged to improve productivity and competitiveness

# Evolution Across Work Programmes
The 2026-2027 WP introduces stronger alignment with the [[Clean Industrial Deal]] and [[Competitiveness Compass]]. Strategic MoU with the automotive sector for R&I in sustainable and smart mobility. Continued emphasis on 35% climate spending target.
