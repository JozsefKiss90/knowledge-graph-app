# Overview
Cluster 1 'Health' is one of six thematic clusters under Pillar II ('Global Challenges and European Industrial Competitiveness') of [[Horizon Europe]]. It funds R&I to support healthcare resilience, leverage biotechnology and AI, and address key public health needs including mental health, disease prevention, pollution- and climate-related health impacts, and the development of critical medicines and medical countermeasures.

# Strategic Role
Cluster 1 contributes to the goal of making the EU the most attractive place for life sciences by 2030, aligned with the [[Strategy for European Life Sciences]]. In the 2026-2027 work programme, it also addresses harmful effects of digital technologies on the mental health of children and young adults.

# Policy Context
The cluster operates within the framework of Europe's Beating Cancer Plan, the [[European Green Deal]]'s health dimensions, and the Commission's priority on healthcare preparedness.

# Horizon Europe Relevance
- Budget contribution to climate action: 16.6% (EUR 176 million) in the 2026-2027 WP
- Supports [[EU Missions]] on Cancer
- Leverages [[Artificial Intelligence]] and biotechnology

# Evolution Across Work Programmes
The 2026-2027 WP expands the scope to include digital mental health interventions and reinforces the connection to the [[Strategy for European Life Sciences]] and the life sciences ecosystem. Increased emphasis on biotechnology and AI-enabled health solutions compared to 2023-2024.

# Implications for Proposal Design
- Proposals should address healthcare resilience and public health impact
- Integration of [[AI in Science]] approaches is increasingly valued
- [[Gender Equality]] dimension mandatory unless topic exempts it
- [[Open Science]] practices required, including FAIR data management
- [[Do No Significant Harm]] principle applies
