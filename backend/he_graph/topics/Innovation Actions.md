# Overview

Innovation Actions (IA) are one of the core action types in [[Horizon Europe]], designed to fund activities directly aimed at producing plans and arrangements or designs for new, altered, or improved products, processes, or services. They cover prototyping, testing, demonstrating, piloting, large-scale product validation, and market replication.

# Funding Rate

Innovation Actions are funded at **70% of eligible costs** (100% for non-profit legal entities). This lower co-funding rate compared to Research and Innovation Actions (100%) reflects the closer-to-market nature of IAs and the expectation that participants contribute more of their own resources.

# Key Characteristics

- Evaluated on Excellence, Impact, and Quality and Efficiency of Implementation
- Stronger emphasis on exploitation, market readiness, and business models compared to RIAs
- [[Dissemination and Exploitation]] obligations are reinforced, including the requirement to use the Horizon Results Platform if results are unexploited after one year
- Intellectual property management strategy must be commensurate with desired outcomes and impacts

# China Exclusion

Legal entities established in China are **not eligible to participate in Horizon Europe Innovation Actions in any capacity**. This includes participation as beneficiaries, affiliated entities, associated partners, third parties giving in-kind contributions, subcontractors, or recipients of financial support to third parties. Exceptions may be granted on a case-by-case basis for justified reasons. The exclusion is justified under Article 22(6) of the HE Regulation, given concerns regarding intellectual property generated under publicly funded programmes and the ongoing EU-China Joint Roadmap discussions.

# Participation Restrictions

Beyond the China exclusion, Innovation Actions are more likely to carry additional participation restrictions:
- Actions related to EU strategic assets, interests, autonomy, or security may limit participation to EU Member States and specific associated countries only (Article 22(5))
- Entities controlled directly or indirectly by ineligible third countries may be excluded
- Additional exploitation obligations may apply for up to four years after the end of the action, linked to restrictions of participation or control due to [[Open Strategic Autonomy]] considerations

# Evaluation Context

Innovation Actions and Programme Co-fund Actions are evaluated under the *Excellence* criterion with consideration of the [[Gender Equality|gender dimension]] and [[Open Science]] practices under methodology. The evaluation template for IAs reflects the closer-to-market positioning with greater weight on impact pathways and exploitation credibility.

# Implications for Proposal Design

- Budget at 70% funding rate requires co-financing strategy
- Exploitation plan and IP management are critical for evaluation
- Verify country eligibility carefully, especially for China-based partners
- Check topic-specific participation restrictions in call text
- Consider [[Innovation Procurement]] (PCP/PPI) as complementary pathway
- Business model and market readiness are more heavily weighted than in RIAs
