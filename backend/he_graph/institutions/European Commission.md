# Overview
The European Commission is the executive arm of the EU and the primary governing institution for [[Horizon Europe]]. It designs and implements the framework programme's work programmes, manages calls for proposals (through executive agencies), conducts evaluations, and oversees grant agreements.

# Strategic Role
The Commission sets the political priorities that shape Horizon Europe's strategic orientations, including the [[European Green Deal]], the [[Clean Industrial Deal]], and the [[Competitiveness Compass]]. It publishes the biennial work programmes (adopted via Commission Decision) that translate strategic orientations into funded actions.

# Horizon Europe Relevance
- Adopts work programmes (e.g., C(2025) 8493 for 2026-2027)
- Manages the Funding & Tenders Portal
- Coordinates with [[European Research Council]], [[European Innovation Council]], [[European Institute of Innovation and Technology]], and [[Joint Research Centre]]
- Conducts ethics appraisal and security appraisal procedures
- Oversees [[European Partnerships]] and [[EU Missions]]
