# Overview

Cybersecurity is a cross-cutting research theme in [[Horizon Europe]], spanning both civilian security and digital industrial domains. It addresses the protection of information systems, critical infrastructure, and democratic processes against cyber threats, and is a key dimension of the EU's [[Open Strategic Autonomy]] ambitions.

# Strategic Role

Cybersecurity sits at the intersection of two clusters:

- **[[Cluster 3 Civil Security for Society]]**: equips security practitioners and critical infrastructure operators with modern tools to counter cybercrime, strengthen the EU's cyber resilience through advanced protective measures, and support the ProtectEU strategy and the European Preparedness Union Strategy.
- **[[Cluster 4 Digital Industry and Space]]**: develops the underlying digital security technologies, secure-by-design architectures, and contributes to open strategic autonomy in digital infrastructure.

The 2026-2027 work programme positions cybersecurity as integral to the strategic orientation of *a more resilient, competitive, inclusive, and democratic Europe*.

# Policy Context

- **ProtectEU strategy**: Cluster 3 directly supports this framework for addressing terrorism and cybercrime threats.
- **European Preparedness Union Strategy**: crisis management and cyber resilience strengthened.
- **Foreign Interference in R&I**: the [[European Commission]] has published a toolkit for tackling R&I foreign interference, with specific recommendations on cybersecurity for higher education institutions and research performing organisations. All [[Horizon Europe]] participants are encouraged to familiarise themselves with this guidance.
- **European Economic Security Strategy**: cybersecurity as a pillar of economic resilience.

# Horizon Europe Relevance

- Funded through both [[Cluster 3 Civil Security for Society]] and [[Cluster 4 Digital Industry and Space]]
- Security appraisal procedure applies to all cybersecurity-sensitive proposals (security self-assessment, security review, security scrutiny)
- Participation restrictions may apply for actions related to EU strategic assets, interests, autonomy, or security (Article 22(5) HE Regulation)
- Ethics review scope explicitly includes data protection, privacy, and dual-use considerations relevant to cybersecurity research
- Classified information handling governed by Commission Decision 2015/444
