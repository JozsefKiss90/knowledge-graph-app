# Overview
Societal Readiness is an approach that aims to improve the adaptation, acceptability, and uptake of innovations by society. It assesses the level of societal adaptation of a project, technology, product, process, or intervention for integration into society.

# Key Dimensions (from Responsible Research and Innovation)
1. **Reflection** — reasoning on goals, values, and motivations of R&I
2. **Inclusion** — involving diverse stakeholders from an early stage
3. **Anticipation** — examining intended and unintended consequences
4. **Responsiveness** — aligning R&I with new perspectives and values

# Policy Context
Introduced as Part 11 in the Programme Guide (V5.0, May 2025). Builds on Responsible Research and Innovation (RRI) framework from Horizon 2020. Complements [[SSH Integration]] and [[Social Innovation]].

# Implications for Proposal Design
- Ensure R&I is driven by societal needs and values
- Build inclusive, multi-stakeholder coalitions
- Identify and mitigate negative externalities
- Integrate societal readiness from the beginning of the innovation process
