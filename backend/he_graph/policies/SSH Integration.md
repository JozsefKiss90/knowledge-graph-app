# Overview
The effective integration of Social Sciences and Humanities (SSH) across all clusters, [[EU Missions]], and [[European Partnerships]] is a principle throughout [[Horizon Europe]] (Recital 14 of the HE Regulation). SSH integration supports interdisciplinary R&I that combines SSH research with STEM to better address complex societal challenges.

# SSH-Flagged Topics
Many topics require SSH contributions and are flagged as 'SSH-relevant' on the Funding & Tenders Portal. These are STEM topics that should be enhanced by insights from SSH disciplines. Proposals under SSH-flagged topics must:
- Meaningfully integrate SSH contributions throughout the project
- Embed participants with SSH expertise in core activities
- Describe SSH methods used

Insufficient SSH integration results in a lower evaluation score.

# List of SSH Disciplines
**Social sciences, education, business and law:**
- Economics, sociology, political science, psychology, anthropology, geography, demography, peace and conflict studies, human rights
- Education science
- Journalism and information
- Business and administration
- Law

**Humanities and the arts:**
- Religion, theology, languages, literature, history, philosophy, archaeology, linguistics
- Fine arts, performing arts, design, crafts

# Policy Context
SSH integration is closely linked to [[Societal Readiness]], [[Social Innovation]], [[Gender Equality]], and inclusiveness as complementary cross-cutting priorities.

# Implications for Proposal Design
- Check if topic is SSH-flagged on the Portal
- Include SSH consortium partners with relevant expertise
- Describe SSH methods and contributions explicitly
- Even non-flagged topics may benefit from SSH integration
