# Overview
Ethical compliance is an integral part of all EU-funded research from beginning to end. [[Horizon Europe]] requires thorough ethical evaluation from the conceptual stage, including adherence to the European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity.

# Ethics Appraisal Procedure
The Ethics Appraisal Procedure covers all HE-funded activities and includes:
1. **Ethics Self-assessment** (applicant, application phase) — Ethics Issues Table
2. **Ethics Screening** (ethics experts, evaluation phase) — flags serious/complex issues
3. **Ethics Assessment** (ethics experts, evaluation/grant preparation) — for hESC/hE proposals or flagged proposals; can result in ethics requirements as contractual obligations
4. **Ethics checks, reviews, and audits** (during implementation)

# Scope of Ethics Review
- Human rights and protection of human beings
- Animal protection and welfare
- Data protection and privacy
- Health and safety
- Environmental protection
- Artificial intelligence
- Research integrity (fabrication, falsification, plagiarism)

# Policy Context
- Grounded in HE Regulation 2021/695 (Articles 18-19)
- ALLEA European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity
- EU Charter of Fundamental Rights
- Ethics requirements become contractual obligations in grant agreements

# Implications for Proposal Design
- Complete Ethics Issues Table during application
- Prepare ethics section in Part B of the Description of Action
- Expect Ethics Review for all above-threshold proposals
- Ethics requirements may need to be addressed before grant signature
- Consider domain-specific guidance (AI ethics, data protection, dual use)
