# Overview
International cooperation in [[Horizon Europe]] involves collaboration with legal entities established in non-EU countries (third countries). Non-EU countries are either associated or not associated to the programme. Currently 22 countries are associated.

# Association Framework
- Governed by HE Regulation 2021/695
- Association via international agreement between EU and non-EU country
- Associated country entities participate under equivalent conditions as EU Member States (with possible limitations)

# Participation Rules
- **EU Member States**: full participation
- **Associated countries**: equivalent conditions
- **Low-to-middle income countries**: automatically eligible for funding
- **Other third countries**: participate at own cost, exceptional funding possible
- **International organisations**: specific rules depending on type
- **China**: entities excluded from Innovation Actions
- **Russia/Belarus/non-government Ukraine territories**: entities excluded from participation (with limited MSCA exceptions)

# Policy Context
The 2026-2027 WP actively encourages international collaboration with particular focus on Africa, Mediterranean, Latin America, Caribbean, India, and South-East Asian nations. Aligned with the Global Gateway and the European Economic Security Strategy.

# Restrictions on Participation
- Actions related to EU strategic assets may limit participation to EU/associated countries only
- Entities controlled by ineligible third countries may face exclusion
- EU restrictive measures (sanctions) apply

# Foreign Interference in R&I
The Commission has published a toolkit for tackling R&I foreign interference, with recommendations on values, governance, partnerships, and cybersecurity for HEIs and research organisations.
