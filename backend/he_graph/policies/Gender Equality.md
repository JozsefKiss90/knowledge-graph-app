# Overview
[[Horizon Europe]] sets gender equality as a cross-cutting principle, aiming to eliminate gender inequality and intersecting socio-economic inequalities throughout R&I systems. The strengthened provisions address three levels: institutional (GEPs), content (gender dimension in R&I), and representation (gender balance).

# Three Pillars of Gender Equality in Horizon Europe

## 1. Gender Equality Plans (GEPs) — Eligibility Criterion
Public bodies, research organisations, and higher education establishments from EU Member States and associated countries must have a GEP in place (as of calls with deadlines in 2022). This is an **eligibility criterion**.

## 2. Gender Dimension in R&I Content — Mandatory by Default
Integration of the gender dimension (sex and/or gender analysis) into R&I content is mandatory across all work programmes, destinations, and topics, unless non-relevance is specified in the topic description.

## 3. Gender Balance — Ranking Criterion
Gender balance among researchers is strongly encouraged and will be taken into account as a ranking criterion for ex aequo proposals. Target of 50% women in HE advisory bodies and evaluation panels.

# Policy Context
- Specific funding for inclusive GEPs under 'Widening Participation and Strengthening the ERA'
- Gender studies and intersectional research funded under [[Cluster 2 Culture Creativity and Inclusive Society]]
- [[European Innovation Council]]: 40% target for women-led companies, 50% women in EIC advisory structures, dedicated women-led deep-tech initiative
- Three gender categories for self-identification: woman, man, non-binary

# Key Concepts
- **Sex**: biological features (chromosomal, hormonal, morphological)
- **Gender**: sociocultural norms, identities, and relations
- **Intersectionality**: overlapping categories (gender, ethnicity, age, socioeconomic status)

# Implications for Proposal Design
- Verify GEP eligibility before submission
- Describe how gender dimension is integrated in R&I content
- Provide justification if gender dimension is not relevant
- Consider intersectionality in research design
- Aim for gender balance in project teams
