# Overview
Social innovation concerns the development of new products, methods, and services for and with society — involving citizens, public authorities, business, industry, and academia (the Quadruple Helix). It is a cross-cutting specific issue in [[Horizon Europe]].

# Strategic Role
Social innovation engages and empowers communities, enhances resilience, increases relevance and acceptance of innovation, and helps foster lasting changes in social practices. In Horizon Europe, it serves environmental, economic, digital, cultural, sovereignty, and democratic priorities.

# Horizon Europe Relevance
- Cross-cutting across all programme parts
- Flagged in specific topics encouraging applicants to consider social innovation
- Dedicated topics for social innovation
- Particularly relevant for health and care, green and digital transitions, climate adaptation, citizen engagement, and deliberative democracy

# Implications for Proposal Design
- Embed social innovation to develop solutions for multiple interconnected challenges
- Involve intended users from the beginning
- Consider social innovation as a way to meet topic objectives
- Address social acceptance and uptake of results
