# Overview
Open Science is a comprehensive policy framework in [[Horizon Europe]] based on open cooperative work and systematic sharing of knowledge and tools as early and widely as possible. It moves beyond open access to encompass a full suite of practices from proposal stage to project reporting.

# Strategic Role
Open science is a cross-cutting principle evaluated under 'Excellence' (methodology) and 'Quality and efficiency of implementation' award criteria. Horizon Europe sets the legal basis for open science obligations and incentives through the HE Regulation and Model Grant Agreement.

# Policy Context
Open science practices in Horizon Europe are divided into:
- **Mandatory practices** (per grant agreement): open access to publications, responsible management of research data (FAIR principles), data management plans (DMPs), reproducibility provisions, access to tools/instruments for validation
- **Mandatory per work programme/call**: additional obligations set in specific calls
- **Recommended practices**: early and open sharing, citizen engagement, open peer review, output management beyond data — incentivised through evaluation

# Horizon Europe Relevance
- Failure to address mandatory open science practices results in lower evaluation scores
- Adopting recommended practices results in higher evaluation scores
- Publications must be immediately open access under Creative Commons licenses (CC BY preferred)
- Research data open by default ('as open as possible, as closed as necessary')
- [[European Open Science Cloud]] is the target infrastructure for FAIR data deposition
- Open Research Europe platform provides open access publishing for all HE-funded research
- ERC programme does NOT include open science in evaluation (exception)

# Key Implementation Mechanisms
- **Open access to publications**: immediate, via trusted repository, under CC BY / CC BY-NC / CC BY-ND / CC BY-NC-ND
- **Research Data Management (RDM)**: mandatory for all projects generating/reusing data; DMP due by month 6
- **FAIR principles**: Findability, Accessibility, Interoperability, Reusability
- **Reproducibility**: transparent research design, preregistration, open software/workflows
- **Open peer review**: encouraged over traditional blind review
- **Citizen and civil society engagement**: co-design, co-creation, co-assessment activities

# Implications for Proposal Design
- Address open science in the Excellence section (methodology) of the proposal
- Describe how mandatory and recommended practices will be implemented
- Identify trusted repositories for data and publications
- Plan DMP early (though formal DMP not required at submission)
- Justify if open science practices are not relevant
- Do not rely on Journal Impact Factor — qualitative assessment of publications used
