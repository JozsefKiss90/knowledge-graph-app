# Overview
The 'Do No Significant Harm' (DNSH) principle originates from the [[European Green Deal]] Commission Communication and is further specified in the EU Taxonomy Regulation (2020/852). It requires that Horizon Europe-funded activities do not cause significant harm to any of six environmental objectives.

# Six Environmental Objectives
1. **Climate change mitigation** — no significant GHG emissions
2. **Climate change adaptation** — no increased adverse climate impacts
3. **Sustainable use and protection of water and marine resources**
4. **[[Circular Economy]]** — no significant waste/resource inefficiencies
5. **Pollution prevention and control** — no significant pollutant emissions
6. **Protection and restoration of [[Biodiversity]] and ecosystems**

# Policy Context
The HE work programme has been co-created to support R&I activities that respect climate and environmental priorities. Applicants can reference the DNSH principle to demonstrate their project will not harm any of the six objectives.

# Horizon Europe Relevance
- **Evaluators will NOT score compliance with DNSH** unless explicitly stated in the work programme
- The DNSH assessment continues to be carried out as a 'do no harm' check
- At least 35% of HE funding allocated to climate objectives
- At least 10% of 2025-2027 budget to biodiversity-related topics

# Implications for Proposal Design
- Reference DNSH when presenting research methodology and expected impacts
- Show that project activities will not significantly harm any of the six environmental objectives
- Not a scored criterion unless topic explicitly states so
- Relevant for demonstrating environmental responsibility
