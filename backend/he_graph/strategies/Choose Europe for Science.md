# Overview
'Choose Europe for Science' is an initiative within the [[Horizon Europe]] Work Programme 2026-2027 designed to make Europe a premier destination for scientific careers. It aligns with broader EU goals of fostering innovation by creating an environment where researchers can thrive.

# Strategic Role
By offering stable funding, excellent research opportunities, and access to cutting-edge facilities, the EU aims to attract and retain top global talent. Building on a 2025 pilot, the initiative promotes research careers that go beyond project-based work.

# Key Components
- **[[Marie Sklodowska-Curie Actions]]**: EUR 50 million for long-term fellowships, postdoctoral career stability, relocation incentives; linking MSCA grants to competitions for long-term positions
- **[[Research Infrastructures]]**: nearly EUR 50 million for strengthening world-class facilities, improving transnational access, training and upskilling
- **ERA Chairs**: EUR 240 million to attract high-level researchers to widening universities/centres
- **[[European Innovation Ecosystems]]**: supporting growth of global, technology-driven companies
- **'Talent ecosystems for attractive early research careers'** topic: promotes the new European framework for research careers
