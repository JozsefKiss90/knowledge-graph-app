# Overview
Horizon Europe (2021-2027) is the EU's key funding programme for research and innovation, with a budget of approximately EUR 95.5 billion (current prices), including EUR 5.4 billion from NextGenerationEU and an additional EUR 4.6 billion reinforcement. Established by Regulation (EU) 2021/695, it succeeds Horizon 2020 and marks a paradigm shift from activity-driven to impact-driven funding.

# Programme Structure
Horizon Europe is divided into **three pillars** and one cross-cutting **part**:

## Pillar I — Excellent Science
- [[European Research Council]] — frontier research
- [[Marie Sklodowska-Curie Actions]] — researcher mobility
- [[Research Infrastructures]] — world-class facilities

# Impact-Driven Design
Work programmes are organised around 'Destinations' (corresponding to expected impacts identified in the Strategic Plan) and 'Topics' (describing expected outcomes and scope). The 32 expected impacts span social, economic, ecological, and scientific aspirations. Key Impact Pathways capture and communicate impact around 9 key story lines.

# Strategic Orientations (2025-2027)
Three overarching, interlinked strategic orientations:
1. **The green transition**
2. **The digital transition**
3. **A more resilient, competitive, inclusive, and democratic Europe**

# Budget (2026-2027 WP)
- Over EUR 14 billion invested in R&I
- 35% to climate objectives (EUR 4,903 million)
- 15.7% to biodiversity (EUR 1,661 million)
- 33% to digital transition
- EUR 2,023 million to AI development
- 50% of call budget via [[Lump Sum Funding]]
