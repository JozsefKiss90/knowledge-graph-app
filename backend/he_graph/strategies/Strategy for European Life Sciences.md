# Overview

The Strategy for European Life Sciences is a key EU strategy supported by the [[Horizon Europe]] Work Programme 2026-2027. It aims to make the EU the most attractive place for life sciences by 2030, encompassing pharmaceutical innovation, biotechnology, healthcare technologies, and the broader life sciences ecosystem.

# Strategic Role

The strategy serves as a policy anchor for R&I activities across multiple parts of [[Horizon Europe]], particularly:

- **[[Cluster 1 Health]]**: R&I activities directly support healthcare resilience, leverage biotechnology and [[Artificial Intelligence|AI]], address key public health needs (mental health, disease prevention, pollution- and climate-related health impacts), and develop critical medicines and medical countermeasures — all contributing to the EU's life sciences attractiveness goal.
- **[[Cluster 6 Food Bioeconomy Natural Resources Agriculture and Environment]]**: actions support the bioeconomy strategy and foster bio-based products and value chains, which are integral components of the broader life sciences ecosystem.

The strategy also connects to the [[Cluster 1 Health|Cluster 1]] objective of developing innovative interventions to prevent harmful effects of digital technologies on the mental health of children and young adults.

# Policy Context

- Part of the broader EU competitiveness and industrial agenda alongside the [[Clean Industrial Deal]] and the [[Competitiveness Compass]]
- Reflects the EU's ambition to compete globally in pharmaceutical and biotech sectors
- Addresses supply chain resilience for critical medicines and medical countermeasures
- Contributes to the strategic orientation of *a more resilient, competitive, inclusive, and democratic Europe*
