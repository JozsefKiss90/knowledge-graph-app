# Overview
Open Strategic Autonomy (OSA) refers to the EU's capacity to act autonomously in key strategic areas while preserving an open economy. In [[Horizon Europe]], it manifests as participation restrictions, strategic technology investments, and supply chain resilience measures.

# Horizon Europe Relevance
- Topic conditions may limit participation to EU Member States or EU + specific associated countries for actions related to strategic assets, interests, autonomy, or security
- EUR 634 million allocated to [[Critical Raw Materials]] topics in 2026-2027
- Investment in critical technologies: energy, [[Quantum Technologies]], advanced manufacturing
- Legal basis: Article 22(5) of the HE Regulation
- [[Cluster 4 Digital Industry and Space]] is the primary vehicle for OSA in digital/industrial domains

# Policy Context
- Aligned with European Economic Security Strategy
- [[Competitiveness Compass]] emphasises OSA
- The second Horizon Europe strategic plan (2025-2027) strengthens OSA narrative
- Geopolitical context: reducing strategic dependencies amid conflicts in Europe and Middle East
