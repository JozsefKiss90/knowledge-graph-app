# Overview
The Clean Industrial Deal is a major EU strategy reflected in [[Horizon Europe]] through a dedicated horizontal call 'R&I in Support of the Clean Industrial Deal' with an indicative budget of EUR 540 million. It aims to accelerate market deployment of cutting-edge clean technologies and decarbonised industry solutions.

# Strategic Role
The horizontal call takes a bottom-up, industry-led approach, facilitating cross-sectoral integration and market-driven R&I responses. It consists of two large, open topics:
1. **Decarbonisation of energy-intensive industries**
2. **Clean technologies for climate action**

The focus is on creating 'fit-for-deployment' projects with robust business and market readiness strategies.

# Policy Context
- Fosters synergies between Horizon Europe and the Innovation Fund
- Ensures continuous pipeline of R&I solutions ready for market deployment
- Seeks to boost European competitiveness, mobilise investments, and reduce energy prices
- Contributes to EU industrial decarbonisation and clean energy objectives
- Clusters 4 and 5 also co-finance the horizontal call
